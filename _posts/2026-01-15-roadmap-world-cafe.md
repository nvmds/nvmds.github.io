Most roadmap sessions involve one person at the whiteboard and everyone else nodding along. A World Café flips that: small groups rotate through stations, each building on what the last group left behind, until the whole room has shaped the direction together.

## Why it works

People commit to what they help create. A roadmap handed down top-down gets debated for weeks after the fact; a roadmap several dozen people helped write in an afternoon tends to just... happen.

## Running the format

- **Stations, not slides.** Each table tackles one theme — goals, risks, dependencies, sequencing.
- **Rotate every 15–20 minutes.** Groups move on, a "table host" stays behind to brief the next group.
- **Converge at the end.** The final round is spent voting and spotting the patterns that showed up at every table.
